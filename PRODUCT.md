# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Recruiters first: they arrive from a CV or LinkedIn, skim for 30–60 seconds, and decide whether to forward. Hiring managers and tech leads are the second read after a forward — slower, more skeptical, looking for depth and judgment. The site speaks to both, but the first impression is tuned for the recruiter's fast pass.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio of Dmytro Platov, Senior Frontend Engineer (React / TypeScript). Success is standing out from the stack: out of 50 candidate portfolios, this is the one the recruiter remembers. It exists to be memorable, not merely to pass a credibility check.

## Positioning

Craft + taste: a senior engineer who also has design judgment — a rare combination, and the site itself is the evidence. Every screen must look like it was designed on purpose by someone who could not have shipped a template.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: LinkedIn connect — move the conversation to where recruiters live. Secondary: the contact form (Telegram-backed).
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: "The React dev whose portfolio actually looked designed — bold, precise, clearly senior."
- Belief ladder: (1) this person has real taste — the hero proves it on sight; (2) senior-level experience is real — experience section with real employers; (3) the work backs it up — live projects with repos; (4) worth connecting now — LinkedIn CTA.
- Proof on hand: deployed projects with GitHub links (projects section), real work history and employers (experience section), and testimonials/references from colleagues that exist but are not yet on the site — collect them into `.impeccable/assets/proof/` when ready to add.

## Brand Personality

Bold, raw, opinionated. Brutalist edge forward: big type, hard contrasts, deliberate roughness. The confidence is in commitment, not decoration — the site takes a position and holds it.

## Anti-references

- The template dev portfolio: particle hero, typewriter intro, identical project cards, skill percentage bars. Anything a recruiter has seen 40 times this week.
- Corporate SaaS landing: gradient blobs, glassmorphism, generic hero + feature grid.

## Design Principles

1. **The site is the CV.** Every visual decision is a work sample. If a section looks default, it testifies against the positioning.
2. **Bold is the register, clarity is the job.** Brutalist moves (scale, contrast, rawness) must never cost the recruiter their 30-second read: name, role, stack, experience, contact stay instantly findable.
3. **Taste first, credentials fast behind.** The hero earns attention on sight; experience and live projects convert that attention into belief before the CTA asks for anything.
4. **Commit, don't decorate.** One accent, hard edges, real content. No ornament that a template could have added.
5. **Memorable beats safe.** When a choice is between "polished but forgettable" and "opinionated with a point of view", take the opinion.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA: ≥4.5:1 body-text contrast in both themes, full keyboard navigation, visible focus states, and `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for all animation. Both light and dark themes must independently meet the bar.
